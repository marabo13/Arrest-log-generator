Article I: Classification of Offenses

All crimes against the Order are classified into three Tiers, determining the severity of the offense and the corresponding range of sanctions.

Tier I: Infractions

Description: Minor offenses that demonstrate a lack of discipline and respect. These are weaknesses to be corrected through punishment.

Standard Sanctions: Reprimand, assignment of possible fines, or brief imprisonment (not to exceed one hour).

Tier II: Transgressions

Description: Serious violations that actively harm the Order, its members, or its goals. These acts demonstrate a CLEAR flawed character and potential for betrayal.

Standard Sanctions: Rank demotion, forfeiture of assets (e.g., credits), temporary suspension from the Order, extended imprisonment, or branding with a Mark of Censure.

Tier III: Heresies

Description: The most severe category of crimes, representing a fundamental betrayal of the Order's principles. These acts are unforgivable and threaten the Order's secrecy.

Standard Sanctions: Rank reset, permanent ban from the Order, declared Kill-on-Sight (KOS), or permanent death (PD).
